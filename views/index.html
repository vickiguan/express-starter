About Me

"Life is like a box of chocolate, when you're allergic to cocoa powder." And that's when my mother first offered me the sweet treat. Right before she pushed me out of the car. All I felt was pain. Lots of pain and the sensation of chocolate. The next thing I knew, I was in pitch-black darkness. It smelt of dead frogs and clean linen. But as it turned out, there were no frogs nor clean linen. Only linen that wasn't so clean.

On the blood stained sheets, laid a tired and confused me. The gruff sounding elderly woman next to me was complaining loudly about how her family hadn't been contacted yet about her broken shins. They were lovely shins. I turned to the other side to find someone of undiscernable gender, race, or biological composition. It proceeded to blush as I decided that the ceiling was the most interesting thing in the vicinity. However, something far less noticeable soon grabbed my attention. My foot was missing.
